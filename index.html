Who we are

About Samarth Foundation

Our mission is to bridge the gap between potential and opportunity — ensuring that every individual, regardless of where they were born, has access to the tools they need to thrive.

Established in 2016, Samarth Foundation is a registered non-governmental organisation (Reg. No. F48181/Pune) headquartered in Pune, Maharashtra. Over nearly a decade of ground-level work across the Khed Taluka region, the Foundation has built a verifiable track record spanning education, healthcare, animal welfare, crisis relief, sanitation and environmental restoration.

Our mission

Empower communities through education, health and sustainable development — fostering growth and self-sufficiency for families.

Our vision

A world where every individual achieves their fullest potential, in resilient communities that adapt and flourish.

Geographic reach

Active across 21+ villages in Khed Taluka — ZP schools, Gram Panchayats and public-health networks.

Our approach

Every intervention starts with on-ground assessment, co-designed with village leaders and principals for lasting ownership.

The need

Why Project Samruddhi, why now

Sanitation gap

  • Open defecation persists in 6 target villages
  • No functional mobility-toilet units today
  • Direct health & dignity impact on women and children

Energy & digital divide

  • 18 ZP schools lack reliable power for digital classes
  • Power cuts disrupt instruction and tools
  • Solar offers permanent, zero-cost energy independence

Infrastructure deficit

  • No transit hubs in Kanersar, Retawadi, Wadgoan
  • Overcrowded classrooms need new rooms urgently
  • No dedicated women's spaces in several villages

Healthcare delivery

  • 50 Arogya Sewikas operate without digital tools
  • Manual records slow diagnosis & treatment
  • Tablets enable real-time data & health tracking

Accountability

Monitoring, sustainability & impact audits

Funding partners receive structured reporting at every stage of the project lifecycle.

Quarterly milestones

Progress reports every quarter with photo documentation, completion status and spend against plan.

Certified audit

Financial-utilisation statements verified by independent certified auditors at each milestone.

Beneficiary feedback

Operational benchmarks gathered directly from village leaders and school principals.

Visual datasets

Before-and-after photo and video documentation of every completed intervention.

Impact metrics

Attendance rates, camp footfall, energy savings and sanitation coverage tracked throughout.

Sustainability plan

Each asset transferred to Gram Panchayats & school committees with a maintenance framework.

Implementation timeline

A 12-month delivery plan, from mobilisation to final impact audit.

1 Month 1–2

Mobilisation & procurement

Ground assessment finalised, vendor empanelment, and material procurement initiated for village infrastructure and solar panels.

2 Month 3–5

Village infrastructure delivery

Mobility toilet units, Ghanta Gadi, Mahila Asmita Bhavans and bus stands completed across 14 village locations.

3 Month 4–7

School interventions

Solar rooftops across 18 ZP schools; classroom construction at Retawadi, Bahul and Dawadi; water filters, computers and washrooms deployed.

4 Month 6

Healthcare enablement

50 tablets procured, configured and distributed to Arogya Sewikas with training on digital health-tracking tools.

5 Month 8

Commissioning & handover

All assets commissioned, formally handed over to local governance bodies, and inaugural events conducted.

6 Month 9–12

Monitoring & impact audit

Independent audit, beneficiary-feedback collection, and a final impact report with visual documentation submitted to the CSR partner.
